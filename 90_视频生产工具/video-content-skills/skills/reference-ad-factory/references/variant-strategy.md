# Controlled variant strategy

Model randomness creates incidental difference, not an editorial strategy. Give every variant a declared communication angle.

## Lock across the batch

- product geometry, operation order, brand rules, product truth, prohibited claims;
- aspect ratio, resolution, caption safe area, loudness, language, and delivery codec;
- character identity when one campaign requires a recurring presenter;
- the reference template's core proof sequence unless a planned test changes it.

## Change deliberately

- hook: pain-result, efficiency, product structure, professional recommendation, before-after;
- application setting, shot size, camera movement, narration wording, proof example, CTA;
- presenter or environment only when campaign continuity permits it.

## Recommended first batch

1. `pain-result`: show the mess and the protected result immediately.
2. `efficiency`: lead with time saved or fewer cleanup steps.
3. `product-structure`: lead with what makes the product different.

Add `professional-recommendation` and `before-after` only after the first three pass quality gates. Compare performance by hook and proof structure, not by random seed.
